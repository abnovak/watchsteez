about: Justin purchased his first watch when he was 14. It was a two-tone Casio for $51.15 and he still has the watch complete with boxes, paperwork, and receipt. Things have changed quite a bit since then. "My first great vintage watch was a Universal Geneve Polerouter Automatic passed down to me from my great grandfather who was a highly- skilled watchmaker." Justin then decided to take two semesters of horology classes classes at MATC in Milwaukee, Wisconsin to learn more about the art of watchmaking and repair. He is a member of the Commonwealth Crew, Chicago's version of the Red Bar Crew in New York where you can meet people that are equally-obsessed with horology.

vision: Where do I buy vintage watches? Who can I trust? Watch collectors frequently ask us these questions. Here at watchsteez.com, our main goal is to bring a collection of tasteful, yet affordable vintage watches to the market. You may also see an occasional modern watch thrown into the mix. Sometimes it's all about style and design.

style guide: Be sure to also visit our style guide. We believe that you shouldn't have to spend a fortune to have great style.
